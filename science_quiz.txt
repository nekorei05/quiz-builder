Life on Earth depends heavily on photosynthesis, the process by which green plants, algae, and certain bacteria convert sunlight into chemical energy. During photosynthesis, chlorophyll absorbs light energy, which is then used to transform carbon dioxide and water into glucose and oxygen. This glucose serves as a vital source of energy for plants and, indirectly, for animals that consume them. Oxygen released during the process replenishes the atmosphere, making respiration possible for most living organisms. Without photosynthesis, the Earth’s ecosystems would collapse, as it is the foundation of nearly all food chains and a key regulator of atmospheric gases.
